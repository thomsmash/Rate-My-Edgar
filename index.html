What Are NFTs?

NFT stands for non-fungible token. They are a type of digital art whose ownership is verified on the blockchain. The blockchain is the technology that allows cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin and Etherium to function, though the major difference being that cryptocurrencies are fungible. This means that one unit of the currency, say Bitcoin, is interchangeable with every other Bitcoin. NFTs use unique addresses on the blockchain to differentiate between each other, making each one a unique digital good.

How Do NFTs Work?

NFTs are sometimes hand drawn or hand created, but most of the NFTs that are currently popular are generated with some form of algorithm. By randomizing the features of each NFT, creators can create hundreds of unique pieces of art with significantly fewer drawings. Once the NFTs are created, they are then "minted" on the blockchain and given a unique address. The blockchain in functionality is a linked database of addresses and transactions, and multiple platforms exist for the purpose of uploading NFTs to the blockchain.

Who is Edgar?

Edgar is a cartoon character created for the purpose of demonstrating NFT generation. Edgar has 9 possible backgrounds, clothings, mouths, eyes, and hairs, each represented by the corresponding 0-8 digit in the 5-digit Edgar ID. Edgar shows how each NFT has a unique address, in this case represented by the Edgar ID. Instead of verifying ownership, Edgar ID just verifies that each NFT is unique.
